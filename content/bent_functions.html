Bent Functions¶

Content partially extracted from data/Bent_Functions.txt. Original page: https://boolean.wiki.uib.no/Bent_Functions

Background and Definition¶

The covering radius bound states that the nonlinearity \(nl(F)\) of any \((n,m)\)-function \(F\) satisfies \({\displaystyle nl(F)\leq 2^{n-1}-2^{n/2-1}}\). A function is called bent when it achieves this bound with equality.

Properties¶

Since nonlinearity is a CCZ-invariant, CCZ-equivalence (and therefore EA-equivalence and affine equivalence) preserves the bent property.

The algebraic degree of any bent \((n,m)\)-function is at most \(n/2\).

An \((n,m)\)-function is bent if and only if all its derivatives \(D_aF\), for \(a\neq 0\), are balanced. In this sense, bent functions are also called perfect nonlinear (PN) functions.

Bent (PN) \((n,m)\)-functions exist only for \(n\) even and \(m\leq n/2\). Conversely, for any pair of integers \((n,m)\) satisfying this condition, there exists a bent \((n,m)\)-function.
