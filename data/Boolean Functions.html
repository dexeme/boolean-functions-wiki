Equivalences of Boolean functions[edit | edit source]

Two 𝑛-variable Boolean functions 𝑓,𝑔 are called affine equivalent if there exists a linear automorphism 𝐿 and a vecor 𝑎 such that

𝑔(𝑥) = 𝑓(𝐿(𝑥)+𝑎).

𝑔(𝑥) = 𝑓(𝐿(𝑥)+𝑎)+𝓁(𝑥).
